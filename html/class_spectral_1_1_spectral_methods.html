static

A static method that computes the differentiation matrices D1, D2, ..., DM on Chebyshev nodes.
The code implements two strategies for enhanced accuracy suggested by W.Don and S.Solomonoff in SIAM J.Sci.Comp.Vol. 6, pp. 1253–1268 (1994). The two strategies are(a) the use of trigonometric identities to avoid the computation of differences x(k) - x(j) and(b) the use of the "flipping trick" which is necessary since sin t can be computed to high relative precision when t is small whereas sin(pi - t) cannot.
J.A.C.Weideman, S.C.Reddy 1998.

nSize of differentiation matrix.

Note: 0 < m <= n-1.

dm[ell] contains ell-th derivative matrix, ell=1..m.
